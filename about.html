Who Are We?


SynBio is a synthetic biology organization under the Bioengineering department that strives to foster an iGEM hacker culture at UC San Diego. Think hackathon. Our vision is to be a project incubator that provides the people and resources to develop undergraduate ideas.


We are an undergraduate organization guided by the following beliefs in:

  1. Open-Source Biology

    We want to foster an open DIY biology community – a collaborative environment for bio-enthusiasts to meet, discuss, and execute projects.

  2. Undergraduate Education

    We want to expose members to experimental and computational concepts within synthetic biology and provide workshops to teach members skills to become researchers.

  3. iGEM DIY-Biology Hacker Culture

    iGEM has a huge presence at world-renown universities, such as MIT (who kickstarted the competition), Harvard, etc. As a top Biology & Bioengineering research institute, we strive to bring the iGEM culture onto the campus of UC San Diego.

Secure Lab Space

A long-term goal of SynBio is to establish a BSL1 lab space on campus or nearby, one equivalent of a high school biology laboratory. This initiative is inspired by the new and world's first public biology lab at our local La Jolla Library (led by the amazing Callen Hyland) and the SD Wet Lab (our local DIY Biology group).
